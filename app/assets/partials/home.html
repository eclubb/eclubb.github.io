Woodlands Trail (Grades K-5)

This curriculum-based youth development program is focused on active cumulative learning rather than on earning the next uniform insignia or emblem. Success is not measured in the quantity of insignia but in the quality of the young man.

The program consists of three age groups: Foxes (Grades K-1); Hawks (Grades 2-3); Mountain Lions (Grades 4-5). Each age group is lead by an adult Trail Guide.

Navigators (Grades 6-8)

The Navigators are lead by a Trailmaster who may also work with Trail Guides to deliver some of the curriculum, and provide oversight of the program as boys learn to lead themselves. This learned leadership comes by accepting responsibilities within the unit and learning to work together as a team. Leadership is also learned through service projects and helping other people.

At this level, a Trailman progresses in rank as he masters outdoor adventure skills and accrues service hours.

Adventurers (Grades 9-12)

At this level, advancement continues to focus on a mix of required and elective badges for skill mastery, but also ups the required level of service provided to others.

Emphasis at this level shifts from advancing in rank to earning three increasingly-difficult awards.

Youth leadership of the troop typically comes from among these young men.
